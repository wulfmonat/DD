1. Water_Mask_X.png — for Muck Boots logic

2. Swamp_Mask_X.png — for slow/mud logic

    White = swamp/muck (slowed, or blocked without boots)
    Black = everything else
    Keep separate from water — your code checks water first, then swamp

3. Collision_Mask_X.png — what the player CANNOT walk through

    White = solid
    Black = walkable
    Include: shop walls, fence lines, tree trunks, woodpile, crates, bench backs, large rocks
    Do NOT include: tan paths, grass, water, swamp, or tree canopies (those are visual only)

4. Depth_Overlay_Mask_X.png — for trees/roofs the player walks under
This is the key for "hang over" trees:

    White = pixels that should draw above the player when they're behind it
    Black = transparent
    Use this for: pine tree tops, big random tree canopies, shop roof overhangs
    You don't need this if your engine does Y-sort with separate canopy sprites — but if you're baking trees into the map, this mask lets you split them at runtime

How to handle walk-behind trees (two easy ways)

Way A — Bake + mask (fastest for you):

    Paint the full tree into the base map
    Copy just the top 60% of the canopy into Depth_Overlay_Mask_X (white)
    In code: draw base map → draw player → if player is behind tree, draw the white area from overlay on top

Way B — Keep canopies separate (cleanest):

    Don't bake big trees at all. Keep Pine_Tree_X and Random_Tree_X as separate sprites
    Place trunk base on Collision_Mask, draw canopy with Y-sort (if player.y > tree.y, draw canopy after player)

I recommend Way B for Maps 1-3 (open maps), and Way A for Maps 4-6 where you want dense baked forests.
